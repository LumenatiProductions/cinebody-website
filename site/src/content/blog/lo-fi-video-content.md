<h4><strong>Getting to Grips With The Future of Video Production</strong></h4>
<p>The data is in and the results are clear: when it comes to video production in 2023 and beyond, there’s only one game in town. Lo-fi content is now so effective it is outperforming even the biggest budget productions.&nbsp;</p>


 

 

 

 
 
 

 

 
 <figure>
 
 
 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 <img src="/blog/lo-fi-video-content/img-1.webp" alt="">

 
 
 
 
 

 
 
 </figure>

 

 
 

 

 
 <p>But first, let’s make sure we’re all on the same page with a quick definition: lo-fi video uses accessible shooting equipment to focus entirely on authentic and human experiences. Most often, it’s produced on a smartphone. It might use platform-native elements like the stickers you see on Instagram Stories. And there won’t be hours of effort put into styling, lighting, scripts, or any of the other hallmarks of so-called ‘professional’ videography.</p>
<p>Because when it comes to lo-fi video, only one thing matters: connection. Anything that comes across as fake should be instantly cut. For millennials and Gen Z, connection trumps perfection…Every. Single. Time.</p>
<p>But why does this matter? In short, lo-fi video is a gamechanger for brands big and small. Video search engine optimization (VSEO) is going to start playing a huge role in search engine rankings, which means the onus is going to be on fast, consistent video production. And nothing’s quicker than pulling out a smartphone and pressing ‘record’.</p>
<p>Lo-fi video is also perfect for <a href="https://www.cinebody.com/cinebody-blog/how-to-make-affordable-video-content-amidst-a-recession">making affordable video content in a recession</a> and great for fuelling <a href="https://www.cinebody.com/cinebody-blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-video-production">remote video production</a>.&nbsp;</p>

 <p>In 2022, the numbers told you everything you need to know. <a href="https://thesocialshepherd.com/blog/video-marketing-statistics">86% of marketers now use video</a>. More than three-quarters (78%) say video has directly increased sales and almost nine in 10 (86%) say video has driven more visitors to their website. On top of that, <a href="https://www.facebook.com/business/news/insights/perfection-fatigued-millennials-gen-z-want-human-video?content">research from Meta</a> found an 84% likelihood that self-recorded, mobile-shot videos would drive more views than studio productions and a 63% better chance they’d drive more sales.</p>
